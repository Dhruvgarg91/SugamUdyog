Insights

The sowing in Rabi is progressing well, with a larger area sown than the previous year, and is expected to promote further expansion in the agricultural sector. The Indian agriculture sector is anticipated to grow by 3.5 percent in FY 2022–2023, according to fiscal policy declarations. India has recently quickly established itself as a net exporter of agricultural goods in addition to satisfying domestic demand. Agricultural exports will total $50.2 billion in the fiscal year 2022–2023. Estimates place the nation's overall food grain production for the Kharif season at 149.9 million tonnes.

Industry Scenario

In the first nine months of the Financial Year 2022–23 (April–December), exports of agricultural and processed food products increased by 13% compared to the same period in FY 2021–22, according to preliminary data released by the Directorate General of Commercial Intelligence and Statistics (DGCI&S).


India is both the world's top producer and exporter of cereal-related goods. The export of basmati rice climbed from $ 2379 million (April–December 2021) to $ 3337 million (April–December 2022) over the first nine months of the fiscal year 2022–23, while the export of non–basmati rice saw an increase of 4% during the same period. Exports of non-basmati rice climbed to $ 4663 million in the first nine months of the fiscal year 2022–23 from $ 4512 million in the same period the previous year.


Union Budget 2023 Highlights:

  • INR 20 Lakh Cr: Agricultural Credit Target with a focus on Animal Husbandry, dairy and fisheries sector
  • Atmanirbhar Bharat Horticulture Clean Plant Program: Outlay of INR 2200 Cr to promote high-value horticulture crops
  • Agricultural Accelerator Fund: To be setup to promote startups in rural India
  • Provision of INR 60,000 cr has been made for the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-Kisan)
  • The Modi government has set aside INR 450 crore for the Digital Agriculture Mission and INR 600 crore for the technology-based promotion of the agricultural industry.
  • The government will launch a subscheme under PM Matsya Sampada Yojana with an outlay of INR 6,000 cr
